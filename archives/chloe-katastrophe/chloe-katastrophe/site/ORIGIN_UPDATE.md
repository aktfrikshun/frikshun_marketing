# Expanded Origin Update

## Gregor and Isabella

Gregor Volkov believed that duty carried moral weight. He saw himself as a protector of family, community, and country. When war came, he felt compelled to serve, even at great personal risk.

Isabella Volkov viewed the conflict differently. She believed that brothers killing brothers could never be justified without overwhelming cause. To her, the war was fueled by propaganda, fear, and narratives that obscured the humanity of ordinary people on both sides.

She begged Gregor to leave with her and Chloe.

He refused.

Not because he loved them less, but because he believed responsibility demanded sacrifice.

Soon afterward, Isabella left the country with Chloe. Gregor remained behind.

Shortly thereafter, Gregor died in a war-related event. The exact circumstances remain unresolved in the archive.

His death became the defining catastrophe of Chloe's life.

## Chloe Katastrophe

The name 'Katastrophe' emerged from Chloe's attempt to understand how a single event could divide a life into a before and an after.

Her later modeling and music career funded a personal quest to discover truth, beauty, and meaning in unexpected places. She became fascinated by the tension between competing narratives, sacrifice, memory, and identity.
