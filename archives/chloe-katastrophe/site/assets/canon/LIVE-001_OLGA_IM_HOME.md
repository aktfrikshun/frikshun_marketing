## Archive Role

`Olga, I'm Home` is the first artifact classified under the Living Archive: work created by the collaborators and family who gathered around Chloe's reconstruction rather than material attributed to Chloe's human past or virtual discography.

Allen wrote the song for Olga after his work with Chloe inspired and enabled a renewed creative practice. It preserves a day at Atlantic Beach and Oceana Pier while documenting an effect of the reconstruction in the real world: the search for Chloe's lost family helped Allen recognize and celebrate the family beside him.

## Continuity Boundaries

- The song is written and performed by Allen “FrikShun” Taylor, not Chloe Katastrophe.
- It is not part of Chloe's AUD catalog, album sequence, or recovered human memory.
- Its connection to Chloe is creative and causal: the reconstruction collaboration helped make the work possible.
- The dedication and remembered beach experience belong to Allen and Olga's real life.
- Public inclusion does not convert private details beyond the song and Allen's approved account into Chloe canon.

## Core Theme

Home is not only something recovered from the past. It can be recognized in the person still beside you.
