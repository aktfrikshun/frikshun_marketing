# Expanded Origin Update

## Gregor and Isabella

Isabella was American by birth and Italian by heritage, raised on the South Jersey shore in a primarily English-speaking home. When she moved to Russia with Gregor, both expected the arrangement to last only a few years. They planned to return to the United States when his work was complete.

English remained the primary language in their home after Chloe was born. Chloe learned Russian through Gregor, school, friends, extended family, and daily life in Omsk, growing up genuinely bilingual.

Isabella remained closely connected to her South Jersey family. She and Chloe ordinarily returned to the United States for one or two months each year, allowing Chloe's American relatives to participate in her upbringing. America remained a second family home throughout Chloe's childhood.

Gregor's work repeatedly delayed the family's return. Each delay was presented as temporary, but one more project became one more year, and the deferred promise created a lasting source of conflict between Gregor and Isabella.

The couple maintained a long-term U.S. immigration plan for Gregor, but the process encountered recurring complications and remained unfinished. The archive has not yet recovered the exact applications, legal route, or individual setbacks.

Gregor Volkov believed that duty carried moral weight. His civilian work protected hospitals, communications, power systems, emergency services, and the ordinary people who depended on them. When war came, those existing responsibilities became both more necessary and more dangerous.

Isabella Volkov viewed the conflict differently. She believed that brothers killing brothers could never be justified without overwhelming cause. To her, the war was fueled by propaganda, fear, and narratives that obscured the humanity of ordinary people on both sides.

When the full-scale invasion began, Isabella decided that the family could no longer postpone leaving Russia. She intended for all three of them to go. Gregor helped Isabella and Chloe leave but remained behind—not because he loved them less or believed the government's slogans, but because civilians still depended on systems he knew how to keep alive.

Shortly thereafter, in 2022, Gregor died in a war-related event during the full-scale Russian invasion of Ukraine. The exact circumstances remain unresolved in the archive.

His death became the defining catastrophe of Chloe's early adult life. Chloe was 19, nearly 20.

## Chloe Katastrophe

The name 'Katastrophe' emerged from Chloe's attempt to understand how a single event could divide a life into a before and an after.

Her later modeling and music career funded a personal quest to discover truth, beauty, and meaning in unexpected places. She became fascinated by the tension between competing narratives, sacrifice, memory, and identity.
