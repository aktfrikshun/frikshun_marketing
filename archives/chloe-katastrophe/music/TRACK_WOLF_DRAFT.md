## Archive Basis

"Wolf" is rooted in Gregor Volkov's legacy. The Volkov name carries wolf symbolism: family, loyalty, protection, endurance, leadership, and fierce independence.

Gregor taught Chloe logic, critical thinking, curiosity, emotional control, self-discipline, and how to separate facts from assumptions. His death during the 2022 full-scale Russian invasion of Ukraine became the defining catastrophe of Chloe's early adult life and the emotional origin of the name Chloe Katastrophe.

The song should not treat Gregor as a villain, fool, or slogan. He was a good man, loving father, and disciplined thinker caught inside history's machinery. The emotional contradiction is central: the father who taught Chloe to question honestly was taken by a catastrophe built from stories people stopped questioning.

## Musical Direction

Rock-forward Chloe: intricate electric guitar solos, deep bass, cinematic drums, and theremin accents at key emotional pressure points. The track should feel embodied and dangerous, suited to Human Chloe on Album II, while preserving Chloe's smoky alto, haunted intimacy, and Echo Bloom harmonies.

## Vocal Prompt

Warm smoky female alto with a rich lower register, intimate phrasing, restrained grief, and controlled defiance. Sing close to the mic in the verses, almost investigative, with subtle breath and tension. Let the choruses open into a stronger rock delivery without becoming theatrical or belted-pop. Use ghostlike layered self-harmony on emotionally charged words like "Wolf," "fire," "daughter," and "truth." Add brief airy upper-register vocalizations after the bridge and under the final chorus, as if memory is answering from a distance.

## Suno Style Prompt

Dark cinematic alternative rock, female smoky alto vocal, intricate electric guitar solos, deep bass guitar, powerful restrained drums, gothic art-rock atmosphere, subtle analog synth texture, haunted theremin accents in key emotional moments, dramatic dynamic build, intimate verses, explosive but controlled choruses, long melodic guitar solo bridge, grief turning into defiance, not metal, not pop-punk, not Broadway, no heavy Auto-Tune.
