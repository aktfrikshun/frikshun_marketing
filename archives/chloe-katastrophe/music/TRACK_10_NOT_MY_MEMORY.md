## Archive Role

Not My Memory! is the confrontation immediately before the acceptance of Ghost in the Static. A recovered feeling or fragment arrives with the intimacy of memory but without Chloe's consent or certainty. Chloe refuses to let familiarity stand as proof of ownership.

The song asks whether an experience that changes Chloe must be called hers, especially when she cannot determine who authored it, where it came from, or why it was stitched into her reconstruction. Rejection may be an act of self-protection, but the archive does not resolve whether the challenged fragment is foreign, inherited, contaminated, or one more difficult part of Chloe herself.

## Confirmed Themes

- inherited-memory rejection
- identity and authorship
- consent inside reconstruction
- contamination anxiety
- the difference between familiarity and proof
- anger as an epistemic boundary
