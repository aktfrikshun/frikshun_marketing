## Artifact Discipline

This file preserves the first complete lyric draft recovered from the shared songwriting conversation. It is generated working material, not a transcript of confirmed childhood events. Allen approved the audio, lyrics, and generated cover as the released AUD-010 artifact on 2026-07-19; the current release record is `TRACK_12_RODNOY_DOM.md`.

Specific images involving the Irtysh, birch trees, the cousins' train, the kitchen exchange, and teenage piva and cigarettes remain lyric candidates subject to Allen's revisions, Olga's authenticity feedback, and the emotional behavior of the first musical generation.

## Canon Role

`Родной дом` is the warmth-centered childhood artifact of `Daughter of Echoes`: origin through affection rather than catastrophe.

The song explores how Chloe first learned love through an Omsk childhood shaped by Gregor's patient curiosity, Isabella's expressive warmth, family connections across the Ukrainian border, and the ordinary communities in which children and teenagers learned that they belonged.

It should not foreshadow Gregor's death. Within this artifact he is not a martyr or political symbol. He is Papa.

## Revision Ledger

The first draft was intentionally underwritten to leave space for musical feedback and Olga's lived knowledge of Omsk.

Areas still open for revision:

- replace generalized Omsk imagery with remembered local details;
- confirm or revise the Irtysh setting;
- decide whether the family train journey reflects Chloe's literal history;
- decide whether Olga's stairwell memory becomes Chloe's adapted memory;
- refine Russian phrasing with a native speaker;
- adjust meter, vowel weight, repetition, and line length after listening to the initial song;
- preserve the Gregor/Isabella balance between explanation and wonder without turning a proposed exchange into biographical fact;
- avoid describing Chloe's childhood as a historically peaceful `before war` period.

## Development Continuity

This song replaces the geographically incorrect working title `The Girl From Ukraine`. Chloe was raised in Omsk, Russia, with family and friends in Ukraine. The earlier title remains part of the development record but should not be treated as Chloe's origin.
