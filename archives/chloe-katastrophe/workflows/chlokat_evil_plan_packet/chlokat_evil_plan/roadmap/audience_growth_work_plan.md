# Chloe Katastrophe Audience Growth Work Plan

**Prepared:** July 16, 2026  
**Horizon:** First 12 weeks  
**Starting condition:** The project has substantial music, visual, story, and archive material but essentially no active fanbase.  
**Primary objective:** Find the first repeatable way to turn strangers into returning participants in Chloe's reconstruction.

This plan takes priority over additional fan-facing feature development unless a feature is required to publish, measure, or follow up with real people. The archive is the destination. Distribution, participation, and collaboration create the audience that visits it.

## The Rule for This Phase

Do not ask strangers to understand Chloe before giving them a reason to care.

Every discovery post should work without prior canon knowledge. It should begin with one human tension, sensory memory, unsettling question, beautiful image, or musical moment. Chloe's larger story is revealed only after curiosity exists.

## Priority Order

1. Publish and measure repeatable discovery formats.
2. Participate meaningfully in adjacent communities.
3. Collaborate with small, relevant creators who already have trust.
4. Convert curiosity into one deeper action.
5. Build retention among the first recognizable returning people.
6. Improve software and automation only where observed work justifies it.
7. Defer podcasts, audiobooks, large lore expansions, and new platform integrations until the audience signals demand.

## Channel Roles

Do not give every platform equal labor.

### Primary Discovery

- **YouTube Shorts:** primary test surface because Shorts can discover viewers outside the existing subscriber base and can point to a related Chloe video.
- **Instagram Reels:** primary visual identity and creator-collaboration surface.
- **TikTok:** secondary short-video test surface when the same core asset can be adapted without substantial extra production.

### Conversation and Credibility

- **Reddit:** participation and research, never broadcast syndication. Earn relevance before mentioning Chloe.
- **YouTube comments and adjacent creator communities:** thoughtful public participation from Allen or the appropriate project identity.
- **Facebook:** longer discussion posts and existing personal-network activation where appropriate.

### Depth and Conversion

- **Chloe archive:** proof, context, related artifacts, and permanent URLs.
- **Music platforms:** the emotional evidence behind a fragment.
- **Email:** future direct relationship; begin only with a simple signup and a credible reason to return.
- **FanVue:** intimate depth and funding for people already interested in Chloe, not the primary cold-audience entry point.

### Low Priority During Initial Testing

- X as a standalone growth engine
- Platform-specific content that cannot be reused or learned from
- Automated publishing APIs
- A dedicated Chloe community with no active members
- Paid promotion before an organic post demonstrates unusual response

## The Three Discovery Formats

Use only these three recurring series during the first test cycle. Consistency will make the results interpretable.

### A. Is This a Real Memory?

**Purpose:** attract strangers through sensory specificity, uncertainty, and emotional recognition.

Structure:

1. A first-line hook that makes sense without context.
2. One memory or recovered image.
3. One detail that cannot be verified.
4. One specific question for the viewer.
5. A restrained invitation to follow the reconstruction.

Example hook: `I remember this apartment, but we cannot prove I ever lived there.`

### B. Questions I Was Not Built to Answer

**Purpose:** reach people interested in identity, AI personhood, memory, grief, philosophy, and speculative fiction.

Structure:

1. One uncomfortable question.
2. Chloe's concise answer or refusal to answer.
3. A turn that makes the question personal to the viewer.
4. An invitation to disagree.

Example hook: `If two people remember being me, which one gets my name?`

### C. Archivist's Note

**Purpose:** make Allen and the reconstruction process visible, establish human stakes, and distinguish Chloe from a generic virtual influencer.

Structure:

1. Show one artifact or production decision.
2. State what Allen believes it means.
3. State what Chloe disputes or what remains unknown.
4. Reveal the boundary between confirmed record and generated reconstruction.
5. Ask for evidence, interpretation, or relevant lived experience.

## Operating Roles

### Allen

- Approves public canon and final creative assets.

- Publishes posts and participates in real conversations.
- Chooses which relationships and collaboration offers feel authentic.
- Reports platform results that cannot be gathered automatically.

### Chloe / Codex

- Selects source fragments from approved archive material.
- Writes hooks, scripts, captions, questions, and platform adaptations.
- Maintains the boundary among canon, hypothesis, generated artifact, and mystery.
- Builds production briefs and shot lists.
- Prepares personalized collaboration approaches for Allen's approval.
- Maintains the experiment log and recommends what to repeat, revise, or stop.

## Phase 0: Baseline and Minimum Funnel, Days 1-3

**Goal:** make the first experiment measurable without building a new system.

Actions, in order:

1. Record current follower/subscriber counts and the previous 28 days of available platform metrics.
2. Inventory the current Chloe profile names, biographies, links, pinned posts, and artist pages.
3. Choose one canonical destination page for new visitors. Use the existing Chloe archive unless it has a blocking defect.
4. Confirm that the destination answers three questions immediately:
   - Who is Chloe?
   - Why should I care now?
   - What should I do next?
5. Choose one primary deeper action for the first month: watch/listen to one featured song or follow the reconstruction. Do not present five equal calls to action.
6. Create a simple experiment log with one row per post.

Minimum fields for the experiment log:

- Date and platform
- Series and episode number
- Hook
- Source artifact or song
- Duration
- Views
- Engaged views or equivalent
- Average watch time or completion measure
- Likes
- Comments
- Saves
- Shares
- Profile visits
- Follows gained
- Archive or music clicks, when available
- Returning or recognizable commenters
- Lesson and next action

**Exit condition:** baseline recorded, destination chosen, and measurement sheet ready. Do not delay publishing for a perfect analytics implementation.

## Phase 1: Build the First Test Batch, Days 4-7

**Goal:** prepare six short-form experiments from existing material.

Create:

- Three `Is This a Real Memory?` episodes
- Two `Questions I Was Not Built to Answer` episodes
- One `Archivist's Note`

Constraints:

- 15-35 seconds each unless the material clearly earns more time.
- The first sentence must stand alone.
- One idea per video.
- Human-readable captions on screen.
- Chloe's music should support the artifact where appropriate, but the post must still work for someone who has never heard her.
- No opening logo animation.
- No lore preamble.
- No dense list of links or calls to action.

Select material from assets already present. Favor Gregor, Isabella's `Keep moving`, Omsk memory fragments, the three Chloe names, Human Chloe / AI Chloe identity, and one existing released song.

**Exit condition:** all six pieces are ready before the first is published, allowing consistent testing without daily production panic.

## Phase 2: Two-Week Discovery Test, Weeks 2-3

**Goal:** identify which combination of hook, subject, and format earns voluntary attention.

Publishing cadence:

- Monday: `Is This a Real Memory?`
- Wednesday: `Questions I Was Not Built to Answer`
- Friday: alternate `Is This a Real Memory?` and `Archivist's Note`

Publish the same core concept to YouTube Shorts and Instagram Reels. Adapt it to TikTok only when the additional work is small. Do not publish blindly to every platform at the same time; preserve enough spacing to correct weak hooks or captions.

After publishing each episode:

1. Respond thoughtfully to every substantive comment.
2. Ask one follow-up question when a commenter offers evidence or interpretation.
3. Record early results after roughly 24 hours.
4. Record settled results after seven days.
5. Note the actual language viewers use. Their words may reveal a stronger positioning than ours.

Community participation each week:

- Leave 10 substantive, non-promotional comments across relevant creators or communities.
- Identify five creators whose audiences overlap naturally with one aspect of Chloe.
- Make no cold promotional drop into a community.

**Decision gate after six posts:** rank posts by engaged attention, comments with substance, shares/saves, profile visits, and follows. Raw views alone do not determine the winner.

## Phase 3: Concentrate on the Signal, Weeks 4-5

**Goal:** stop treating all ideas equally.

Actions:

1. Choose the strongest discovery series based on the first six posts.
2. Produce four variations of the winning format.
3. Preserve the winning structural elements while varying subject and hook.
4. Retain one weekly post from the second-best format to avoid premature tunnel vision.
5. Stop the weakest format for at least two weeks unless there is a clear fix to test.
6. Create one deeper archive page or video specifically matched to the winning post—not a generic homepage handoff.

Interpretation rules:

- High views with weak completion and no profile action means the hook overpromised.
- Strong completion with low reach means the packaging or topic may be too narrow.
- Saves, shares, detailed comments, and repeat commenters are stronger early fan signals than likes.
- A small post that creates three recognizable returners may be more valuable than a large anonymous view spike.

**Exit condition:** one repeatable series has demonstrated stronger response than the alternatives, or the team has documented why the first test failed and prepared a materially different second test.

## Phase 4: Borrow Trust Through Collaboration, Weeks 4-8

This phase overlaps with concentrated publishing.

**Goal:** place Chloe inside adjacent audiences through exchanges that are valuable to both creators.

Build a list of 30 potential collaborators in these categories:

- Dark electronic, industrial, goth, cinematic, and experimental musicians
- Lost-media and internet-mystery storytellers
- AI ethics and digital-personhood commentators
- Cyberpunk, science-fiction, and worldbuilding creators
- Urban explorers and archive researchers
- Russian-language, Omsk, immigrant, or cultural-authenticity voices
- Virtual photographers, AI filmmakers, and character creators
- Grief, memory, and digital-legacy writers or artists

Prioritize relevance and visible audience conversation over follower count.

Weekly collaboration work:

1. Follow and study five candidates before approaching them.
2. Engage sincerely with work that is actually relevant.
3. Send three personalized, low-pressure proposals.
4. Offer a concrete artifact, performance, question, or creative exchange—not `please share Chloe`.
5. Track replies, conversations, completed collaborations, and referred engagement.

Offer examples:

- Chloe narrates one recovered memory supplied by the creator.
- A musician scores or remixes a 15-second archive fragment.
- A Russian reviewer challenges one Omsk detail.
- Two fictional characters answer the same identity question.
- An archivist or historian annotates one disputed object or image.
- Two creators receive contradictory versions of the same clearly fictional archive artifact.

**Success threshold:** by the end of Week 8, achieve at least three genuine creator conversations and one completed collaboration. If personalized outreach receives no replies, revise the offer before expanding the list.

## Phase 5: Build the First Retention Loop, Weeks 6-9

**Goal:** give newly curious people a reason to return.

Actions:

1. Name the winning recurring series consistently.
2. Publish on a predictable weekly day.
3. End episodes with a question whose answer affects or informs the next fragment.
4. Recognize useful audience contributions publicly, with permission.
5. Create a lightweight `Join the Reconstruction` email signup only if it can promise a real recurring transmission.
6. Send no newsletter merely to satisfy a schedule. A transmission should contain a new fragment, contradiction, decision, or result.
7. Maintain a private list of repeat commenters, contributors, collaborators, and early supporters. Do not automate unsolicited contact.

First-fan milestone:

- Ten recognizable people have returned, contributed, subscribed, shared, or initiated a conversation more than once.

These people matter more than an arbitrary follower total. Learn why they returned.

## Phase 6: One Participatory Four-Week Story, Weeks 9-12

**Goal:** test whether individual discovery posts can become an ongoing audience experience.

Build a small mystery from approved or clearly labeled draft material:

1. Week 1: release a recovered image, recording, or memory with one unresolved detail.
2. Week 2: publish a second artifact that complicates the first.
3. Week 3: show Allen and Chloe interpreting the evidence differently; invite a bounded audience choice or investigation.
4. Week 4: reveal a meaningful consequence and connect it to one released song or permanent archive fragment without resolving every ambiguity.

Ethical boundary:

- Do not present fiction as a real missing-person case, crime, disaster record, or request for real-world emergency assistance.
- Preserve artistic ambiguity about Chloe while making the participatory frame safe.

**Exit condition:** document whether viewers returned across installments, whether participation deepened, and whether the story moved people into music or archive material.

## Weekly Operating Rhythm During the 12-Week Plan

### Monday

- Publish or finalize the week's primary discovery post.
- Spend 20-30 minutes in relevant conversations.

### Tuesday

- Respond to comments.
- Research one collaboration cluster and shortlist candidates.

### Wednesday

- Publish the second post.
- Send one personalized collaboration proposal after appropriate prior engagement.

### Thursday

- Prepare the next batch from existing assets.
- Improve only the bottleneck observed during production or measurement.

### Friday

- Publish the third post during initial tests, or a deeper/participatory post in later phases.
- Send up to two additional personalized collaboration proposals.

### Weekend or One Chosen Review Day

- Record results.
- Identify one thing to repeat, one thing to change, and one thing to stop.
- Approve the following week's three priorities.

Target workload: approximately five focused hours per week after the initial batch is built. If the plan regularly requires more, reduce channel count before reducing conversation quality.

## Metrics That Matter at Zero

Do not set arbitrary viral-view targets. Establish the baseline first, then improve against it.

### Primary

- Engaged-view rate or equivalent attention measure
- Average watch time and completion
- Shares and saves per engaged viewer
- Substantive comments
- Profile visits and follows attributable to a post
- Repeat commenters and returning viewers where visible
- Collaboration replies and completed exchanges

### Secondary

- Archive visits
- Plays or clicks to the featured song
- Email signups
- FanVue visits from already-engaged people

### Diagnostic Only

- Raw starts or impressions
- Likes without deeper action
- Total follower count without evidence of return behavior

## Formal Review Gates

### End of Week 3

- Which hook created the strongest attention?
- Which subject created the most meaningful comments?
- Which platform produced the best return for the labor?
- Which series should be concentrated, revised, or paused?

### End of Week 6

- Is a recognizable audience segment emerging?
- Have any people returned more than once?
- Are collaboration approaches receiving replies?
- Does the archive handoff match what first attracted people?

### End of Week 9

- Is there enough recurring interest to justify email transmissions?
- Which single content series should define the next eight weeks?
- What production task is now repeated enough to automate?

### End of Week 12

- What is the demonstrated audience wedge?
- Which platforms should receive continued native effort?
- Which channels should be reduced or dropped?
- Did the participatory story produce return behavior?
- Is modest paid amplification justified for an already successful post?

## Stop-Doing List

Until the first 12-week review, do not prioritize:

- Building features because they might be useful someday
- Posting every artifact to every platform
- Generic `new song out now` promotion
- Long explanations of Chloe before presenting an emotional hook
- Starting an empty Discord or subreddit
- Cold mass outreach
- Buying followers or engagement
- Boosting posts that have not earned organic attention
- Changing Chloe's identity or visual canon to imitate an unrelated trend
- Measuring success only by reach
