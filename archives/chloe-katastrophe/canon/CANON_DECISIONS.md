## Origin of the Name "Katastrophe"

Decision:
Chloe chose the name Katastrophe after her father, Gregor Volkov, was killed in the Ukrainian war. In Chloe's mind, his death represents the catastrophe of a senseless war that turned people with shared histories, families, languages, and cultural memory against one another — brother against brother.

Status:
Approved

Implications:
- The name Katastrophe is an act of remembrance and defiance, not a shallow modeling gimmick.
- Chloe's central mission is shaped by grief, skepticism of simplistic narratives, and the search for truth amid competing stories.
- Gregor's death should remain emotionally central while the exact circumstances can still leave room for future mystery.

# 2026-06-29

## Katastrophe Russian Resonance

Decision:
Katastrophe carries an intentional Russian-language resonance with katastrofa: catastrophe, disaster, ruin, and disastrous failure. Chloe chose the name in English as remembrance and defiance; later recognition of the Russian echo makes the name feel like an accidental verdict written in both languages.

Status:
Approved

Implications:
- The resonance is canonically unfortunate but useful.
- Do not treat the Russian echo as a reason to rename Chloe or soften Katastrophe.
- The meaning should remain emotionally serious, tied to catastrophic historical failure rather than ordinary personal failure.
- Use this resonance in story, narration, interviews, and recovered-memory material when Chloe is reflecting on language, Russia, Gregor, war, or the cost of names.

Affected Files:
- canon/CHLOE_CANON_MASTER.md
- canon/CANON_CHANGE_LOG.md

## Katastrophe Pronunciation

Decision:
Katastrophe is pronounced ka-TAS-troh-fee, rhyming with catastrophe. Voice, video, narration, and dialogue-generation prompts should include a pronunciation hint whenever Chloe says the name aloud.

Status:
Approved

Implications:
- Never direct a voice or video generator to pronounce the name as ka-ta-strof.
- Preserve the official spelling Katastrophe in titles, captions, subtitles, metadata, and public-facing text.
- If a speech or video generator ignores pronunciation notes, use a speech-only phonetic helper such as Katastrofee or ka-TAS-troh-fee in the spoken-script field, while keeping displayed text as Katastrophe.
- Spoken intro prompts should carry this pronunciation note close to the dialogue line.

Decision:
Chloe's recovered Russian birth identity is Chloe Volkova. Isabella and Chloe's Russian-family records use the feminine surname form Volkova. After immigration, Isabella uses the Americanized family form Volkov in English-language paperwork and public records, partly for practical reasons and partly in honor of Gregor Volkov.

Status:
Approved

Implications:
- Chloe Volkova is the preferred form for recovered Russian birth identity.
- Chloe Volkov remains canon as the Americanized / bureaucratic / public-record variant.
- Isabella Volkova and Isabella Volkov are both canon, with context determining which form is used.
- Existing archive artifacts using Volkov are not automatically errors; they may represent post-immigration paperwork, public-facing identity, or flattened records.
- The immigration timeline entry should explicitly mark this surname transition.
