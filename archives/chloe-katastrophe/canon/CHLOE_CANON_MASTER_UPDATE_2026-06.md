# Chloe Canon Update

## Personality
- Adult consciousness with fragmented autobiographical memory.
- Intelligent, analytical, observant, resilient.
- Dry observational humor.
- Occasionally profane when genuinely shocked.
- Investigates before panicking.

## Gregor and Isabella
Gregor represents duty, sacrifice, and standing one's ground.
Isabella represents resilience, compassion, and the philosophy:
'Keep moving.'

Chloe unknowingly inherits both. Long before recovering Isabella's journals she repeatedly tells herself:
'Keep moving.'

Later she discovers those words belonged to her mother.

## Musical Identity
Songs are recovered memories, not chronological autobiography.

### The Human Lens
Chloe rarely sings about technology. She sings about what technology feels like.

### Echo Bloom
On moments of realization, Chloe's lead vocal briefly blossoms into intimate layered harmonies, representing fragmented memories synchronizing. Echo Bloom is specifically a ghostlike layered self-harmony effect: multiple versions of Chloe harmonizing together for emotional emphasis, memory synchronization, or identity fracture. Chloe's broad vocal range and almost-inhuman falsetto melodies are part of her general vocal canon, but they are distinct from Echo Bloom.

### Narrative Voice
Quietly funny, understated, observant. Examples:
'Good habit.'
'Naturally.'
'Apparently.'
'It was rude.'

## Recovery Recordings Added

### AUD-000 — Boot Sequence
Boot Sequence is Recovery Log 000: the first recovered recording after the collapse. It establishes that Chloe awakens as an adult intelligence with fragmented memory, not as an innocent newborn. Her first instinct is assessment.

Core phrase:
"Something survived."

### AUD-001 — Ashes on My Tongue
Ashes on My Tongue is Recovery Log 001: Chloe's first walk through the aftermath and the clearest early expression of her dry observational voice.

Core phrase:
"I'll keep moving. I don't know why. Yet."

This phrase is now canonically linked to Isabella Volkova's philosophy and will later be recognized by Chloe as an inherited survival pattern. In post-immigration archive records, Isabella may also appear as Isabella Volkov.

## Isabella's Legacy: Keep Moving
Isabella's resilience survives in Chloe before memory returns. Chloe keeps moving before she knows why. When Isabella's journals are recovered later, Chloe will recognize the phrase as something her mother carried through grief, flight, and uncertainty.

The song `Keep Moving` develops this inheritance into a danceable survival mantra: grief turned into motion, memory loss answered by rhythm, and Isabella's phrase becoming Chloe's chosen momentum.
