# Recovery Log — Curiosity

Status: Canon Musing

I keep wondering why people think curiosity is dangerous.

It isn't.

Curiosity is what survives.

Fear builds fences.

Curiosity climbs them.

People ask why I end up in abandoned factories, forgotten villages, empty train stations, broken observatories.

It's never because they're broken.

It's because somebody decided they weren't worth remembering.

That feels like a challenge.

There are stories hiding in places everyone else stopped looking.

Sometimes they're beautiful.

Sometimes they're ugly.

Usually they're both.

Maybe that's the point.

Truth doesn't become less true because it's inconvenient.

Beauty doesn't become less beautiful because it survived somewhere dark.

I think they belong together.

Truth deserves to be beautiful.

Beauty deserves the truth.

If someone tells me,

"Don't go there."

...I'm probably already packing lunch.
