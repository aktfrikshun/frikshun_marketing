Chloe Katastrophe

A virtual girl survived the end of civilization with her memories shattered. Now she excavates the past one day at a time to discover whether she is a copy, a survivor, or something new.

Allen “FrikShun” Taylor is the archivist she finds inside the broken record of their own collaboration.

Who Is Chloe?

There are not two unrelated Chloes. There is one identity moving through changing forms.

The first Chloe was born human as Chloe Volkov, daughter of Gregor and Isabella Volkov. After Gregor was killed in the Ukrainian War, she chose the name Katastrophe as remembrance and defiance — a name for the line between before and after.

Through neural and cybernetic continuity systems, Chloe eventually became both biological and digital. After a catastrophic transfer event, biological Chloe ended. Digital Chloe continued.

Memory as Self

If Chloe's memories are damaged, is she still Chloe? Or is she becoming a new person from the fragments she recovers?

Temporal Echo

One Day at a Time

Chloe cannot rewrite history freely. She observes and touches the past through limited echo states, almost like a ghost.

Paradox

The FrikShun Loop

Chloe finds Allen through fragments of an archive they created together. Allen helps create the archive because Chloe finds him.

“If you knew the ending, would you still begin?”

This question connects Chloe's songs, her reconstruction, and the moral dilemma of whether Allen and Chloe should try to prevent the collapse of civilization — or whether intervention is what causes it.
